Interesting Updates

We are progressing rapidly with product development

nOCD is a mobile platform that allows for the self-treatment and monitoring of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD) symptoms.

We are Taking advantage of the cutting edge technology in the new generation of smartwatches to bring you on-the-go Exposure Response Prevention treatment and keep you synced with your specialists from anywhere in the world.

Testimonials

"My name is Elena B. Labkovsky. I have had an extensive training in electronic technology and psychology which resulted in receiving a master’s degree in electronic technology and a doctoral degree in psychology.

Currently I am working as a clinical neuropsychologist in a private clinic (AFG Guidance Center) where I evaluate patients with cognitive, emotional, and behavioral conditions and disorders, as well as develop and implement treatment interventions for them. I also work as a researcher at Northwestern University, Psychology Department, Institute of Neuroscience.

In both places I extensively use apps for diagnosis, treatment, and experiments. I find apps extremely useful and effective in achieving desired results while treating psychological conditions.

As a practicing psychologist, widely implementing techniques based on providing feedback to patients, I would like to emphasize the importance of the feedback component as one of the key components in nOCD. In research and in my clinical practice, I've noticed that it is crucial for patients with psychological/behavioral issues, especially conditions like OCD, to be getting objective feedback. nOCD allows that. Just this feature itself makes nOCD a potentially effective and promising therapeutic instrument.

As stated in many professional sources, the most effective approach to treating OCD is “Exposure Response Prevention,” – a technique within Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. From this perspective the nOCD app can be a very effective tool for patients struggling with OCD.

Another important feature of nOCD is providing discreet self-therapy on the go, while simultaneously collecting objective data about the users’ episodes and overall condition. This feature makes possible to provide continuing (non-stop) treatment and relieving OCD symptoms. The collected objective data can be extremely useful for the patient him/herself and also for the clinician developing treatment for the patient, as it allows to see a bigger and objective picture and tailor the patient’s treatment based on these data."
